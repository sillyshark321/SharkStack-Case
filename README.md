## Materials List
| Material | Count | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **3 mm or 1/8" thick, 24" x 12" sheet of acrylic** | 1 | |
| **M2 x 12 mm standoffs** | 14 | You need 4 extra for the OLED cover (mounted to PCB)<br>I used 11 mm tall standoffs, works just fine |
| **M2 x 8 mm screws** | 22 | You need 28 without tenting |
| **M2 x 18 mm screws** | 6 | Only for tenting, adjust screw lengths if you wish to add more tenting layers |
| **Adhesive rubber feet** | 8 | Optional but highly recommended |

## Assembly
Finish your Corne v3 PCB first - I used [this guide](https://github.com/keyhive/build_guides/blob/master/docs/keyboards/crkbd-v3.md) from KeyHive

Download a .dxf file ([no OLED version](https://github.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/blob/main/dxf/sharkstack_noOLED.dxf) | [with OLED version](https://github.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/blob/main/dxf/sharkstack_withOLED.dxf)) and cut the patterns on your acrylic sheet. Make sure it is 3 mm thick.

Attach 4 standoffs to the outer sides of the base pieces, and screw them in with your short M2 screws.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6298.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6299.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

\
Stack your tenting layers (3 on each side in the cut file), and secure them to the base with the rest of your standoffs and your longer M2 screws.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6301.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6302.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

\
Add 2 big wall layers and 4 short wall layers through the standoffs, as shown here.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6303.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6304.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

\
Add your desired switches onto your plate layers, and mount the Corne v3 PCBs to the switches. Put them in the case.\
Solder PCBs are recommended, but hotswap PCBs work fine too as long as you press firmly. Also attach your OLED covers with your extra standoffs if needed.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6306.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6307.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6308.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6309.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

\
If you wish to use the version with no OLED cover, you may need to trim the pin headers, as this would happen otherwise.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/old/2024-09-08%2023.44.jpeg" width="70%">
</div>

\
Add the last wall layer, and add the top layer on top of the standoffs, and secure the case with the rest of your short M2 screws. Add adhesive rubber feet to finish it off.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6310.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6311.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6312.jpg" width="45%">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sillyshark321/SharkStack-Case/refs/heads/main/img/IMG_6313.jpg" width="45%">
</div>

\
Repeat for the other half of your keyboard. Now you are done!
